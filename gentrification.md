# Neighborhood Gentrification Prediction Model

## Overview

This project explores whether machine learning can identify early indicators of neighborhood gentrification in Chicago using historical real estate transaction data and neighborhood level characteristics. I also explore if there any emerging characteristics that are not detected such as culture or norms. 

## Motivation

Growing up in Chicago and working in the mortgage and real estate industry motivated me to better understand how neighborhoods change over time and whether predictive analytics could help identify early signs of gentrification before those changes become more visible.

## Methods

For this project, I used Random Forest, a tree-based machine learning algorithm that performs well with complex and nonlinear datasets. The model was trained using historical housing and neighborhood data from areas that had already undergone the gentrification process.

## Variables Examined

- Virtual jobs
- Property sales prices
- Transaction timelines
- Neighborhood characteristics (# of cafes, boutiques, etc.)
- Emerging business activity 
- Housing trends

## Goals

The goal of this project is to identify patterns that may signal early neighborhood change and provide insights that could support urban planning, housing research, and business/community decision making.
