# About Me

I am a PhD researcher with a background in data science, predictive analytics, human behavior modeling, and urban systems. My work focuses on using machine learning, statistical modeling, simulation, and spatial analysis to better understand complex social and environmental systems.

My research experience includes predictive analytics, Random Forest machine learning models, spatial statistical analyses such as Local and Global Moran’s I, generalized linear models (GLMs), mixed effects models, and agent-based modeling. I have worked with large scale behavioral, environmental, ecological, spatial, and real estate datasets across interdisciplinary research projects.

Currently, I am developing a neighborhood gentrification prediction model using historical real estate transaction and neighborhood level data to identify early indicators of urban change in Chicago. I also conduct research involving human behavior, socio-ecological systems, and spatial mimicry patterns across residential landscapes.

In addition to my research background, I previously worked in the mortgage and real estate industry for over ten years, which provided practical experience working with consumer financial data, lending processes, and housing market trends within highly regulated environments.
