# Free-Roaming Dog Population Management Modeling

## Overview

This project focuses on modeling free-roaming dog populations and evaluating population management strategies within socio-ecological systems. The research integrates ecological, behavioral, and cultural variables to better understand how human behavior and management decisions influence dog population dynamics.

## Motivation

Free-roaming dog populations create complex interactions between humans, wildlife, public health, and community management practices. This project was designed to better understand how different management approaches may influence long term population outcomes while incorporating human behavior and cultural perspectives into the modeling process.

## Literature Review and Model Development

As part of this research, I conducted a literature review examining previous agent-based models and population management approaches used for free-roaming dog populations across different geographical, ecological, and social systems. This review helped identify commonly used management strategies, behavioral assumptions, and ecological variables that informed the development of the simulation model.

The literature review also highlighted how cultural and community level differences influence management outcomes, which helped guide the integration of socio-ecological variables into the modeling framework.

## Methods

The project incorporates simulation based modeling and statistical analyses to evaluate how different management strategies affect population dynamics over time.

Approaches included:

- Agent-Based Modeling
- Generalized Linear Models (GLMs)
- Behavioral analyses
- Population simulations

## Data Sources

The research integrates multiple forms of data, including:

- Ecological observations
- Human behavior and survey data
- Cultural perspectives
- Population management data

## Goals

The goal of this research is to investigate evidence based decision making for free-roaming dog population management while considering community cultural norms and views.

## Tools Used

- R
- Agent-Based Modeling
- GLMs
